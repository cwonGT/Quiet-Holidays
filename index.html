When are you travelling?

Bay of Plenty

Known as ‘the winterless North’ for its subtropical climate, Northland’s expanses of white sandy beaches, great fishing and scenic locations.

Auckland

The largest Polynesian city in the world and the most multi-cultural. All of which adds up to one big, bustling, cosmopolitan and vibrant place.

Otago

Otago offers a distinct South Island lifestyle and an alternative to more heavily populated northern areas.

Manawatu-Wanganui

Located in the lower North Island, the region is named after its two main rivers.

These are the most popular cities in the Auckland region.

Auckland

Rated as the third most liveable city in the world, Auckland is an exhilarating mix of natural wonders and urban adventures.

Waiheke Island

With pristine beaches, world class wines, and fantastic cuisine, it’s easy to see why Waiheke is considered the jewel in the Hauraki Gulf’s crown.

Manukau

Manukau is the heart of Auckland’s south, a vibrant cosmopolitan centre and home to the largest Polynesian community in the world.

North Shore

Exploring the underground tunnels, gun emplacements and fortifications at North Head is a must.
